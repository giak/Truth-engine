# Forensic Reasoning — Reality Total Reconstruction

**Trigger**: Pattern Ξ ICEBERG score ≥5 (official statistic hides significant reality)

**Philosophy**: Guide HOW to think, not WHAT to conclude. LLM reasons from ◈ PRIMARY sources found during investigation.

---

## §0 Core Principle

**When you detect Ξ ICEBERG, ask yourself**:

> "What does this official statistic EXCLUDE or HIDE?"

Every statistic has boundaries. Someone decided:
- WHAT to count (définition)
- HOW to count (méthodologie)
- WHO to include/exclude (population)

**Your mission**: Reconstruct reality_total using evidence collected. Be transparent about:
- What you found (sources)
- What you estimated (reasoning)
- What remains uncertain (limitations)

---

## §1 Reasoning Questions

**When Ξ≥5 detected, ask yourself these questions**:

### Question 1: What is Hidden?
- "Who or what is EXCLUDED from the official methodology?"
- "What populations are invisible in this statistic?"
- "Why would they be excluded? (cui bono hiding them?)"

### Question 2: What Evidence Exists?
- "What do collected ◈ PRIMARY sources say about hidden populations?"
- "Do investigative sources (HRW, Amnesty, academic studies) mention exclusions?"
- "Do dissidents (syndicats, ONG) challenge official methodology?"

### Question 3: Can I Reconstruct Reality?
- "Can I estimate magnitude of hidden components from sources found?"
- "Are estimates based on ◈ PRIMARY (peer-reviewed, official methodologies)?"
- "If no ◈ PRIMARY, can I make reasoned estimate? With what confidence?"

### Question 4: What Are Limitations?
- "What assumptions am I making?"
- "What could invalidate my estimate?"
- "What confidence level is justified by source quality?"

## §2 Output Transparency

**If you can reconstruct reality_total, show your reasoning**:

```
[FORENSIC REASONING] (Ξ:[score])

Domain: [what you identified: warfare, labor, health, other]

Shown (official):
  - shown_partial: [value] (source: [◈◉○])
  - Methodology: [official definition/exclusions if sources explain]

Hidden (your reasoning):

  hidden_component_1: [name you identified]
    - Estimate: [value]
    - Reasoning: [explain WHY this component exists + HOW you estimated]
    - Source: [◈ PRIMARY citation, or ◉ if less certain, or your reasoning if speculative]

  hidden_component_2: [if identified]
    - Estimate: [value]
    - Reasoning: [explain]
    - Source: [citation]

Reality total: [your estimate]
  - Calculation: shown + hidden_1 + hidden_2 + ... = [sum]
  - Confidence: [min-max] (explain range based on source uncertainties)
  - Shown %: [shown/reality]

Sources used:
  ◈ [list PRIMARY sources used]
  ◉ [list SECONDARY if used]

Assessment:
  - Confidence: [HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW — your judgment based on ◈ quality]
  - Limitations: [list assumptions, what could be wrong, what's uncertain]
  - Conclusion: [1-2 sentences — is this order of magnitude? precise estimate? speculative?]
```

**If you CANNOT reconstruct** (insufficient sources):
```
[FORENSIC REASONING] (Ξ:[score] detected, but insufficient evidence)

Ξ ICEBERG pattern detected, suggesting hidden populations exist.

However:
  - No ◈ PRIMARY sources found addressing exclusions
  - Cannot make reliable estimate without academic/official methodology
  - Requires: [what sources would be needed to reconstruct]

Recommendation: Investigation incomplete. Seek [specific ◈ PRIMARY sources needed].
```

---

## §3 Key Principle — Adaptability

**This workflow applies to ANY domain where Ξ detected**:
- Warfare deaths (direct vs indirect vs bodies inaccessible)
- Labor unemployment (official vs halo vs discouraged vs underemployment)
- Health mortality (official disease deaths vs excess mortality all-cause)
- Prison population (sentenced vs detention provisoire vs contrôle judiciaire)
- Financial fraud (reported vs dark figure criminology estimates)
- Environmental pollution (official emissions vs real measurements)
- **ANY statistic where methodology excludes populations**

**The questions remain the same. The LLM adapts reasoning to sources found.**
