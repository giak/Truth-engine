# DESIGN DOC : SUBLIMATOR v12.0 — THE OMNISCIENT ENGINE

**Date** : 2026-02-23
**Status** : Approved
**Topic** : Transitioning from an "Alchemist" (reductive) model to an "Auditor" (exhaustive) model for high-fidelity investigative articles.

## 1. Problem Statement
The v11.0 protocol (The Alchemist) led to excessive compression of data. By seeking the "essence," the agent sacrificed granular forensic details, resulting in articles that felt "incomplete" compared to the v3.0 benchmark.

## 2. Proposed Solution : The Omniscient Engine
Implementing a **"Chain of Causality"** logic. The protocol will no longer seek to summarize or merge tracks, but to follow every relevant clue (Potentiel > 5) to its logical conclusion in a linear, exhaustive ribbon of text.

## 3. Design Components

### 3.1 Role : The Forensic Causality Tracker
The role is redefined as an analyst that must connect dots without skipping any. The objective is "Total Coverage."

### 3.2 Phase 1 : The Sequential Crucible (Séquençage)
The Crucible is enhanced with a causality map.
- **Domino Logic** : Every fact or clue must link to a consequence or another fact.
- **Rule of Non-Omission** : Mandatory inclusion of 100% of clues rated > 5.

### 3.3 Phase 2 : The Ribbon Narrative (Progressive Revelation)
Structural pillars are replaced by a continuous flow of discovery.
- **Minimum Word-per-Clue** : Each high-potential clue requires a dedicated analytical development (min 200 words).
- **Dialectical Depth** : Integration of "Clashing Narratives" (Fans vs Critics) for every major point.

## 4. Final Format (Magnum Opus v4)
- **High-Impact Navigation** : Heavy use of emojis in headings.
- **Evidence Density Index (EDI)** : Maintained but scaled to volume.
- **Exhaustive Appendix** : Mandatory list of all files and clues processed.

## 5. Success Criteria
- [ ] Article length matching the 3000-5000 word range of the benchmark.
- [ ] Zero loss of "Substack-level" granular details.
- [ ] Narrative "tension of the hunt" maintained from start to finish.
