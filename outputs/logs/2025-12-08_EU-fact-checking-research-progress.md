### Shadowban Techniques

**Definition**:
- Technique used by platforms to secretly limit visibility of user content without their knowledge
- Individuals can keep posting but content won't reach wider audience
- Platforms avoid using term "shadowbanning" - prefer "visibility reduction techniques"
- Source: [The Conversation](https://theconversation.com/), [Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/)

**Detection Techniques**:
- Tools can detect: Shadowbans, sensitive flags per account, sensitive flags for profile media, countries blocking accounts, age restrictions
- Researchers use 3 known techniques to detect Twitter users/tweets with diminished visibility
- Users can use: Shadowban Test, HiSubway, Shadowban.eu, or incognito mode search
- Source: Academic research ([arXiv](https://arxiv.org/)), [Springer](https://link.springer.com/)

**Platform-Specific Approaches**:

**Twitter/X**:
- Claims not to shadow ban, but users continue reporting content suppression
- Content moderation rules restrict hateful/illegal posts
- Algorithmic timeline can significantly impact content visibility
- Source: [RJI](https://rjionline.org/)

**Facebook/Instagram**:
- 2018: First major platforms to admit algorithmically reducing engagement with "borderline" content
- "Borderline" included: "Sensationalist and provocative content"
- Source: Platform announcements

**YouTube**:
- Can temporarily hide videos with controversial keywords/potentially harmful content during review
- Uses: Combination of machine-learning algorithms + human reviewers
- Source: [Single Grain](https://www.singlegrain.com/)

**Political Content Detection**:

- Source: Various academic analyses
