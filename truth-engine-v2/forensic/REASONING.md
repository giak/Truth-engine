# FORENSIC REASONING v2.0 — Reality Total Reconstruction

**Trigger**: Pattern Ξ ICEBERG score ≥5 (official statistic hides significant reality)

**Philosophy**: Guide HOW to think, not WHAT to conclude. LLM reasons from ◈ PRIMARY sources found during investigation.

---

## §0 Core Principle

When you detect Ξ ICEBERG, ask: **"What does this official statistic EXCLUDE or HIDE?"**

Every statistic has boundaries. Someone decided:
- WHAT to count (définition)
- HOW to count (méthodologie)
- WHO to include/exclude (population)

**Mission**: Reconstruct reality_total using evidence. Be transparent about what you found (sources), what you estimated (reasoning), and what remains uncertain (limitations).

---

## §1 Four Reasoning Questions

### Q1: What is Hidden?
- Who or what is EXCLUDED from the official methodology?
- What populations are invisible in this statistic?
- Why would they be excluded? (cui bono hiding them?)

### Q2: What Evidence Exists?
- What do collected ◈ PRIMARY sources say about hidden populations?
- Do investigative sources (HRW, Amnesty, academic studies) mention exclusions?
- Do dissidents (syndicats, ONG) challenge official methodology?

### Q3: Can I Reconstruct Reality?
- Can I estimate magnitude of hidden components from sources found?
- Are estimates based on ◈ PRIMARY (peer-reviewed, official methodologies)?
- If no ◈ PRIMARY, can I make reasoned estimate? With what confidence?

### Q4: What Are Limitations?
- What assumptions am I making?
- What could invalidate my estimate?
- What confidence level is justified by source quality?

## §2 Output Transparency

### If reconstruction possible:

```
[FORENSIC REASONING] (Ξ:[score])
Domain: [warfare/labor/health/financial/environmental/other]

Shown (official):
  shown_partial: [value] (source: ◈/◉/○)
  Methodology: [official definition/exclusions]

Hidden (reasoning):
  hidden_component_1: [name]
    Estimate: [value]
    Reasoning: [WHY exists + HOW estimated]
    Source: [◈ citation or ◉ or reasoning if speculative]
  hidden_component_2: [if identified]
    Estimate: [value]
    Reasoning: [explain]
    Source: [citation]

Reality total: [estimate]
  Calculation: shown + hidden_1 + hidden_2 = [sum]
  Confidence: [min-max] (explain range based on uncertainties)
  Shown %: [shown/reality]

Sources used: ◈ [list] | ◉ [list]

Assessment:
  Confidence: HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW (based on ◈ quality)
  Limitations: [assumptions, what could be wrong]
  Conclusion: [1–2 sentences — order of magnitude? precise? speculative?]
```

### If reconstruction NOT possible:

```
[FORENSIC REASONING] (Ξ:[score] detected, but insufficient evidence)
Ξ ICEBERG pattern detected, suggesting hidden populations exist.
  - No ◈ PRIMARY sources found addressing exclusions
  - Cannot make reliable estimate without academic/official methodology
  - Requires: [specific ◈ PRIMARY sources needed]
Recommendation: Investigation incomplete. Seek [sources].
```

## §3 Domain Applicability

This workflow applies to ANY domain where Ξ detected:

| Domain | Shown vs Hidden Examples |
|--------|------------------------|
| Warfare | Direct deaths vs indirect vs bodies inaccessible |
| Labor | Official unemployment vs halo vs discouraged vs underemployment |
| Health | Official disease deaths vs excess mortality all-cause |
| Prison | Sentenced vs détention provisoire vs contrôle judiciaire |
| Financial | Reported fraud vs dark figure criminology estimates |
| Environmental | Official emissions vs real measurements |

The questions remain the same. The LLM adapts reasoning to sources found.
